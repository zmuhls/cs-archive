PROGRAMME—Continued.
HIGH SCHOOL CONGRESS.

Thursday Morning July 1, 11 o'clock.

W. K. WICKES, Principal of Syracuse High School, Chairman.
G. A. LEWIS, Secretary.

1. Greeting.
2. Pupil—Pleas. For Athletics.
For Instruction in General History.
For Instruction in Civics.
3. A Resolution.

Resolved, That we recommend to the State Department of Educational Economy for the purpose of diffusing among the public school teachers of the Empire State all possible practical information on the following great educational subjects:

LABOR—The Pupil.
CAPITAL—The Teacher.
THE WORKSHOP—The School.
RAW MATERIAL—Text-Books.
TOOLS—Brains.
THE PRODUCT—Knowledge.

Friday Morning, July 2, 10 o'clock.

1. The Art of Expression.
What Kind of Books to Read—Supt. J. G. Rices, of Plattsburg.
The Place and Use of Essay Writing—PROF. BRANARD KELLOGG, of Polytechnic Institute, Brooklyn.
The Teaching of Public Speaking in the Secondary Schools—PROF. DUNCAN C. LEE, of Cornell University.
