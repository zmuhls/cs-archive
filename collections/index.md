## [Local District Governance Records]({{ '/collections/local-district-governance/' | relative_url }})

**708 pages from 1810s-1930s**

Board meeting minutes and governance documents showing decision-making processes across rural and suburban districts. This collection enables comparison of governance evolution across 100+ years:

**Sub-collections:**

- **Amityville Board of Education Minutes** (665 pages, 1930s, Series A4456)
  - Suburban/consolidated district governance
  - Depression-era school administration
  - Formalized board procedures and committees

- **South-Kortright District Minutes** (43 pages, 1810s-1820s, Series A4645)
  - Rural one-room school governance
  - Early common school administration
  - Informal governance structures

**Research Value:** Compare rural (South-Kortright 1810s) vs. suburban (Amityville 1930s) governance, track bureaucratic modernization, study Depression-era school finance.

## [District Administrative Data & Statistics]({{ '/collections/administrative-data/' | relative_url }})

**179 pages of reference materials**

Statistical data, registries, and reference materials for quantitative and geographic research on district evolution:

**Sub-collections:**

- **District Notecard Index** (63 pages, Series B0594)
  - Registration and name change records
  - Administrative actions documentation
  - Index cards from NYS Education Department

- **County Consolidation Tables** (116 pages, Series B0494)
  - Statistical data on district mergers by county
  - Central Rural Schools, Union Free Schools, Town School Units
  - Quantitative consolidation analysis across 29 counties

**Research Value:** Spatial analysis of consolidation patterns, quantitative research on district mergers, geographic visualization of education reform.

## Why This Organization?

### Governance vs. Administration Split

**Governance Records** (Collection 2) show decision-making processes: meeting minutes, debates, votes, committee reports, and temporal narratives of school board actions.

**Administrative Data** (Collection 3) show outcomes and statistics: reference materials, lookup tables, registries, and data sources for quantitative research.

This distinction mirrors archival practice (correspondence vs. registers), enables different research methods (qualitative vs. quantitative), and separates process from product.

## About the Collections

These collections were digitized from archival materials held at the NYS Archives and other repositories. Documents have been processed using multimodal AI (Qwen VL Plus) for transcription, classification, and metadata extraction.

Each item includes:

- High-resolution source images (via GitHub LFS)
- Full OCR transcription
- Document classification and metadata
- Confidence scores for transcription accuracy
